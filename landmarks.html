Famous Landmarks of France

Explore the architectural marvels that define French history and culture.

Eiffel Tower Louvre Museum Palace of Versailles Mont Saint-Michel

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is an iconic symbol of France, located in Paris. Designed by Gustave Eiffel and completed in 1889, it stands as a testament to French engineering and artistry.

Louvre Museum

As the world's largest museum, the Louvre houses masterpieces like the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo. The iconic glass pyramid marks its entrance, blending modern and classical architecture.

Palace of Versailles

This grand palace, built by Louis XIV, showcases the opulence of French royalty. Its gardens and Hall of Mirrors remain a major tourist attraction.

Mont Saint-Michel

Located on a tidal island, Mont Saint-Michel is a medieval marvel with stunning architecture and breathtaking views of the surrounding coast.
